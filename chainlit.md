# Welcome to the Chatty Assistant! 🚀🤖

Nice to have you here!

## How citations work

Every factual statement in an answer is backed by a numbered reference such as **[1]** or **[2]**.
Citations can come from two kinds of sources:

- **Documents** — excerpts from the knowledge base (research papers, reports, etc.). The full
  source details (title, author, date, page) are listed in the **Sources** section at the bottom
  of each response, and clicking a reference opens the full excerpt in the side panel.
- **Tools** — live data retrieved at query time, for example the vacation days tool. These
  references display the tool's name and the result it returned.

### ⚠️ — unsubstantiated claim

When the assistant makes a factual claim that is **not covered by any retrieved document**, it
appends a ⚠️ marker directly after the sentence instead of a numbered reference.
This signals: *"the assistant stated this without a retrievable source — treat it with extra
caution."*

Sentences that explicitly state the documents do not contain certain information are transparent
refusals, not claims, and carry no marker.
